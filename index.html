MAHENDRA SINGH DHONI

Personal Life

Our favourite "Captain Cool", is also called as "MSD" , "Thala" and "Mahi".
He is an Indian cricketer, born in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), on 7th July 1981.
He was the son of Devki Devi and Pan Singh.
An amazing cricketer he is, he started focusing on cricket in 10th standard as he was interested more in Badminton and Football (he was the goalkeeper of his School- Jawahar Vidya Mandir's football team).
He started playing cricket by a lucky chance when his football coach sent him as a wicketkeeper of the cricket team of a local club.
He charmed everyone with his performance and secured a permanent spot as the regular wicketkeeper in the Commando cricket club team for three years during 1995-98.
He continued to perform well and was selected for the Vinoo Mankad Trophy Under-16 Championship team during the 1997-98 season.
He started to take cricket seriously only after completing 10th standard.

Early Career

MS Dhoni started his cricketing career at the age of 17 years from the year 1998 he started playing junior cricket in Bihar.
He became a part of Bihar cricket team. MS Dhoni made his Ranji Trophy for Bihar in 1999- 2000 as an 18 year old.
He made 68 runs in his debut match against the Assam Cricket team, and closed the season with scoring 283 runs in 5 matches.
Start of the ODI career:
The Indian ODI team in the early 2000s saw Rahul Dravid as the wicket-keeper to ensure that the wicket-keeper spot didn’t lack in batting talent.
The team also saw the entry of wicket-keeper/batsmen from the junior ranks, with talents like Parthiv Patel and Dinesh Karthik named in the Test squads.
With Dhoni making a mark in the India a squad, he was picked in the ODI squad for the Bangladesh tour in 2004/05.
Dhoni did not have a great start to his ODI career, getting run out for a duck on debut. Inspite of an average series against Bangladesh, Dhoni was picked for the Pakistan ODI series.
Beginning of the Dhoni Era:
The year 2005 – 2006 marked the beginning of the Dhoni era, where he played many international matches, with Veteran cricketers Like Sachin Tendulkar and others. He was also selected to head the 2007 International World cup, though India Lost in the 2007 world cup, but the potential of Dhoni was realised.
Match Ban:
Although being a success in his field, there was a time when he faced ban from two matches in the December of 2009.
He was suspended for two ODI matches against Sri Lanka by ICC match referee Jeff Crowe as India was three over’s short of the specified rate. Virender Sehwag acted as captain for the two matches in Cuttack and Kolkata.

Achievements of Captain Cool

  1. MS Dhoni is the only captain to win three different ICC Trophies – ICC World T20 in 2007, ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011 and ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.
  2. Dhoni is the only wicketkeeper to effect 100+ stumpings in the ODI format (123). His tally of 195 stumpings in International cricket is also the highest for any wicketkeeper.
  3. MS Dhoni is the only player in the history of ODI cricket to score multiple centuries while batting at No.7 or lower position.
  4. India won each of the eight Test matches played against Australia at home when MS Dhoni was the captain. No other captain has led his team in more home Test matches against an opponent with cent win percentage.
  5. MS Dhoni finished off as many as 13 successful chases in International cricket with a six. Nine of them came in ODI cricket only which are the most by any player where the data is available. He also finished off three successful T20I chases and a successful Test chase by hitting a six.
  6. MS Dhoni has led India to 21 Test victories as captain on home soil which are the most for any Indian captain. He also led India to 74 International victories at home which are also the most for an Indian skipper.
  7. MS Dhoni played his last ODI match as captain at the age of 37 years and 80 days which makes him India’s oldest ODI captain. It was incidentally his 200th match in ODI cricket making him the first Indian captain with 200 ODI caps and only the 3rd captain overall. Dhoni’s 110 ODI wins are the 2nd most for a captain.
  8. Dhoni hit the winning shot in as many as 43 chases in International cricket. 30 of them came in the ODI cricket itself while 10 and three respectively in the T20I and Test formats.

The Indian Premier League- IPL

Dhoni was contracted by the Chennai Super Kings for US$1.5 million. This made him the most expensive player in the IPL for the first season auctions.
Under his captaincy, Chennai Super Kings won the 2010, 2011, 2018 and the 2021 Indian Premier League titles and the 2010 and 2014 Champions League T20 titles.
MS Dhoni became the first player to play 200 T20 matches for Chennai Super Kings.
The former India captain is already on top of the list of most matches played in the Indian Premier League.
Mahendra Singh Dhoni is also one of the only three captains to have won the Indian Premier League twice, with Chennai Super Kings.
In 2013 Chennai Super Kings was banned from IPL for 2 years by R.M. Lodha committee of supreme court of India. It was banned due to the illigal betting of their official Gurunath Meiyappan. He was arrested on the charges of spot-fixing.
According to Mahendra Singh it was darkest period of his career. He said “The biggest crime that I can commit is not a murder, it is actually match-fixing”.
In the 2018 IPL season, Chennai Super Kings returned to IPL, and Dhoni was again selected to lead the franchise. Dhoni scored 455 runs in the tournament and led his side to their third IPL title.
